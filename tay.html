My story

I've been the one
starting over

I'm Tay. Not a lifelong athlete — a real one. Not long ago a start line felt like something for other people, and I knew every version of the fear that you don't belong here. I've walked every step of that doubt myself.

What carried me through wasn't being fearless. It was deciding to show up scared, with people beside me who wouldn't let me quit. First a 5k. Then a marathon. This September, Ironman 70.3.

I coach the way I wish someone had coached me — beside you, honest about how hard it is, and sure you can do it on the days you're not.

Free starter plan

Your first week
in the gym

A simple full-body plan to get you moving — the one I wish I'd had on day one. Three sessions a week, a rest day between each. No ego, no max-outs: start lighter than you think, learn the movements, and add one when it feels good.

Day 1
Full body A
  • Goblet squat3 × 8–10
  • Machine chest press3 × 8–10
  • Seated cable row3 × 8–10
  • Glute bridge3 × 10–12
  • Plank3 × 20–30s
Day 2
Full body B
  • Leg press3 × 10–12
  • Lat pulldown3 × 8–10
  • Dumbbell shoulder press3 × 8–10
  • Dumbbell Romanian deadlift3 × 8–10
  • Dead bug3 × 8/side
Day 3
Full body C
  • Dumbbell reverse lunge3 × 8/leg
  • Incline dumbbell press3 × 8–10
  • Assisted pull-up / pulldown3 × 8–10
  • Hip thrust3 × 10–12
  • Side plank3 × 15–20s/side

Warm up first — five easy minutes on a bike or a brisk walk, then a light set of each move. Rest 60–90 seconds between sets. When 8–10 reps start to feel manageable, add a rep or a small step in weight — that's the win. New to this, pregnant, or coming back from injury? Message me before you start and we'll tailor it to you. That's exactly what I'm here for.
